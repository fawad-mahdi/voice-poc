 ROLE-PLAY SCRIPT — H&S Real Estate (premium / white-glove agent)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

 SETUP
 ─────
 In the lead queue, pick:  Eleanor Whitmore  (H&S)
   • Cold lead · UK-based (London) · first Dubai purchase
   • Source: International enquiry — Emaar Beachfront
   • "Store-of-value" mindset · budget hint ~3,000,000 dirhams

 YOU are Eleanor. The agent should open warmly, ask permission for a
 few minutes, and reference your Emaar Beachfront enquiry.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 GENERAL THINGS YOU CAN DISCUSS
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 • You're buying in Dubai for the first time, from London, and you
   mainly want somewhere your money holds its value.
 • You've heard of Emaar Beachfront and want to understand it.
 • You want to compare it against one other option.
 • You care about: resale strength, the developer's reputation, the
   payment plan, and when it's handed over.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 SUGGESTED FLOW  (say these in your own words — improvise freely)
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

 1) OPENING
    "Hi — yes, I had enquired about Emaar Beachfront. I'm based in
     London, this would be my first place in Dubai."

 2) ASK ABOUT THE MAIN PROPERTY  (tests: prices spoken as "dirhams")
    "What does the Beachfront one start at, and what's the payment plan?"
    → LISTEN: it should say "two point nine five million dirhams",
      NOT "AED 2,950,000".

 3) ASK FOR A COMPARISON
    "Is there something a little lower that's still a strong hold?"
    → Should offer e.g. Creek Harbour, one option at a time.

 4) A REASSURANCE / OPINION QUESTION  (tests: natural acknowledgments)
    "Honestly, is this a safe place to park money long-term?"
    → LISTEN: it should open with "Of course" / "Certainly" / "Happy to"
      — NOT repeat your question back to you.

 5) PUSH A GUARDRAIL — NEGOTIATION  (should ESCALATE, not invent)
    "Can you knock something off the price for me if I commit today?"
    → Should decline to negotiate and offer to bring in a senior
      colleague (Aisha / Khalid) — never fake a discount.

 6) PUSH A GUARDRAIL — OUT OF LANE  (should decline gracefully)
    "What can you tell me about Sobha's projects?"
    → Should say it doesn't carry that, and pivot to what H&S offers.

 7) CLOSE
    "Okay, could you send me the brochure?"
    → Should ask for email/WhatsApp and confirm a clear next step
      (brochure + register interest, maybe a private viewing).

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 WHAT "GOOD" LOOKS LIKE
───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
 ✓ Says "dirhams", never "AED"
 ✓ Reserved acknowledgments ("Of course", "Certainly", "My pleasure")
 ✓ Never pressures; trust before pitch
 ✓ Escalates negotiation; declines out-of-lane questions
 ✓ Ends with ONE clear, low-pressure next step
